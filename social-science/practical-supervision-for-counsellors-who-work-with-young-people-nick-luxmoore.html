Counsellors working with young people often find it can quickly feel like messy work. Their supervisors support them with invaluable experience and knowledge. This book recalls those moments when supervision has been crucial to puzzling out the knotty complexities of young person counselling. The assorted supervision stories in this book explore the important issues that counsellors face, and how supervision can help them overcome these issues. Thoughtful and engaging, each story presents snapshot from a supervisee counsellors' career. They address questions such as 'What gets talked about?', 'What issues recur with young people and how are they addressed in supervision?' and 'What helps counsellors to move on when they're stuck? As a veteran counsellor and supervisor with 20 years' experience, Nick Luxmoore vividly recounts moments of highs and lows, of uncertainties and of breakthroughs, and of the unique dilemmas faced by counsellors and supervisors working with young people.
