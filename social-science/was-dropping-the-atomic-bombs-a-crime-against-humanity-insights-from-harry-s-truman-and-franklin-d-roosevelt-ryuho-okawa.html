Today, Japan faces several interlocking threats. The concrete threat of communist China's military expansion accompanies renewed threats to Japan's global image. We have seen a resurfacing of the fabricated claims that the Japanese Imperial Army used Korean comfort women in World War II, as well as false accusations that Japan conducted mass killings in the Nanking Massacre during the Sino-Japanese War. Historically, Japan has been portrayed as part of the imperialistic and fascist faction of the Axis Powers. The image of Japan as having an "evil history" has created a dangerously dire lack of self-respect in the Japanese people and Japanese leaders. It has contributed to weakening the Japan Self Defense Force even in the face of repeated military threats by North Korean missiles. Perhaps most importantly, this constructed image of Japan has formed the basis of the United States' justification of its World War II atomic bombing of Hiroshima and Nagasaki. Was there any true justification for these bombings? To answer to this question, Master Ryuho Okawa conducted spiritual interviews with Harry S. Truman and Franklin D. Roosevelt, the two presidents who presided over the United States' participation in World War II. Could anything justify the use of nuclear weapons on civilians? Was Pearl Harbor really a sneak attack, or did Franklin Roosevelt know of it beforehand? This book reveals valuable information that will help the world gain a truthful understanding of world history.
